Hook Head Lighthouse

Guarding the Seas of County Waterford since the 13th Century

Lighthouse Tour

Step back in time on a guided tour of the medieval tower. Climb the 115 spiral steps to the top and be rewarded with a breathtaking panoramic view of the rugged coastline. Our knowledgeable guides will share captivating stories of lighthouse keepers and the rich maritime history of this iconic beacon.

The tour offers a unique glimpse into the daily life and heroic deeds of the men and women who maintained the light, ensuring safe passage for sailors for over 800 years. It’s a fascinating journey for all ages, combining history, engineering, and stunning natural beauty.

Visitor Centre and Café

After your tour, relax and refuel at our on-site café. Enjoy a selection of freshly brewed coffee, homemade baked goods, and light lunches while overlooking the dramatic seascape. The café is the perfect spot to unwind and reflect on your adventure.

Our visitor centre also features an interactive exhibition detailing the lighthouse's construction and its role throughout history. Explore exhibits showcasing the evolution of lighthouse technology and discover the secrets of this ancient monument at your own pace.

Coastal Walks and Wildlife

The area around Hook Head is a paradise for nature lovers. Follow the marked coastal paths that wind along the cliffs, offering unparalleled views of the Irish Sea. Keep an eye out for diverse marine life, including seals, dolphins, and a variety of seabirds that call this coastline home.

The walking trails are suitable for all fitness levels and provide a refreshing outdoor experience. Don't forget your camera to capture the dramatic scenery and the serene beauty of the County Waterford coastline.
